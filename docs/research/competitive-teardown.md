# 21 — Competitive Teardown (synthesis)

Synthesizes the competitive landscape for **language-agent** — a chat tutor that teaches reading + writing through a conversation that **blends English with the target language, starting English-heavy and gradually integrating more target language as the learner masters words**, with a margin channel, click-to-explain replies, and post-turn correction, all produced inside an agentic loop.

Built from the teardown artifacts (`research/04-teardown-reading-apps.md`, `research/05-teardown-chatbot-tutors.md`) plus every other research file that names real products (02 blend, 03 SRS, 09 dictionary, 10 TTS, 11 correction).

**Verdict up front.** The market splits cleanly into two camps that never meet: (1) **assisted readers** (LingQ, Readlang, Satori, Du Chinese, Migaku, Yomitan, Language Reactor) that have nailed per-word knowledge status, context glosses, and audio over *static, single-language* text — but do no conversation and no writing correction; and (2) **chat/speaking tutors** (Langua, Speak, TalkPal, Quazel, Duolingo Max, Praktika) that have solved post-turn correction, click-to-explain, and level adaptation — but are *speaking-first and target-only immersion*. **No surveyed product deliberately ramps L1→L2 density as a designed axis.** The two camps each own half of what we need; nobody combines reading + writing + chat + a margin + a gradually-integrating blend. The blend is our wedge; everything else is a known, copyable pattern.

### One-look takeaways from the matrix
- **Per-word knowledge status is the most-copied mechanic** in the reading camp (LingQ status ladder, Lute 0–99, Satori knowledge-sync, Migaku/Yomitan for i+1). It drives *both* SRS *and* how text renders. This is the exact data structure our blend selector needs.
- **Post-turn, categorized, on-demand correction is solved** (Langua's pencil). We copy it wholesale; we do not reinvent it.
- **Click-any-word → meaning + save** is now table stakes in *both* camps (LingQ, Readlang, Satori, Yomitan, Langua). The differentiator is *context-aware sense selection* (Satori hand-picks; Readlang/Language Reactor do it with AI) plus **morphological breakdown + TTS** in one panel.
- **The blend column is almost all ✗ or ~.** Only Toucan/Beelinguapp/Satori-furigana touch graded bilingual density, and all three stay shallow precisely to dodge the grammatical-coherence problem. **The middle of that column is empty — that's the gap.**

## Patterns to STEAL

1. **Persistent per-word knowledge status on every token** (LingQ / Lute / Satori). One status enum — `new → recognized → familiar → known → ignored` — that (a) feeds SRS, (b) controls scaffolding density, and (c) **licenses a word into the target-language stream once it's mastered**. One model, three payoffs. Back it with FSRS `(S, D, R)` so the legible status and the scheduler never disagree.
2. **Context-aware sense selection, never a dictionary dump** (Satori editors → Readlang/Language Reactor AI). Our agent already knows the sentence it generated, so passing context to the gloss step is nearly free. Showing the *wrong* sense is worse than no gloss.
3. **Density as a first-class, dial-able control** (Kindle Word Wise 5-step Fewer↔More slider; Toucan less/many/more; Satori furigana-by-knowledge). Expose a manual assistance slider layered *under* the automatic blend — control without fighting the spine. A humane "more English" panic button.
4. **Lookup → SRS is one motion** (Readlang auto-flashcard, Yomitan one-click Anki, LingQ auto-LingQ, Langua saved-words-re-enter-chat). No friction between "I looked this up" and "I'll review it" — but auto-prune `Known` to avoid review debt.
5. **Post-turn, on-demand, *categorized* correction** (Langua pencil → "Check & explain"; ERRANT-style `op:pos:subtype` codes). Don't interrupt flow with inline red. Recast + one-line rule as the safe default; gentle/explicit as a setting; an **"Explain My Answer" deep-dive** (Duolingo) on each correction.
6. **Remediation generated from the learner's own error history** (Langua drills, Speak "Made For You"). Keep an error ledger keyed by ERRANT code; escalate a recurring code from gentle gloss → mini-rule → targeted practice.
7. **Layered marginalia, not inline-crammed** (Satori "Other Notes" pop-ups + underlined "more here" spans). Keeps the reading line clean. This *is* our margin channel's interaction model: L1 glosses early, migrating to L2, plus grammar nutshells and light encouragement.
8. **Karaoke word-highlight synced to audio** (Beelinguapp; enabled by Azure WordBoundary / Polly Speech Marks / ElevenLabs char-timestamps). Per-word audio AND per-sentence, adjustable speed (Satori). This *is* the reading-practice surface.
9. **Display adapts to the individual learner's known-set** (Satori "according to my knowledge"). Two users at the same nominal blend ratio should see *different* sentences because their known-word sets differ. The blend is per-reader, not per-lesson.
10. **Open data ownership / export** (Lute self-host, Yomitan AnkiConnect). Offer `.apkg` export via `genanki` (L2 front / L1 + breakdown back + TTS media) as a trust feature for a solo project that might grow — without trying to *be* Anki.

## Anti-patterns to AVOID

1. **Naive token-swap blending → incoherent "Frankenstein" sentences** (the #1 risk; the reason Toucan stays at single-word, grammar-untouched swaps). Never post-hoc find-replace. The agent generates *whole* coherent sentences at the target blend level, then a cheap validation pass rejects ungrammatical mixes before the payload ships. Switch at constituent boundaries, not mid-phrase.
2. **Wrong-sense glosses on polysemous words** (bare dictionary lookup). Actively misleading. Always pass sentence context to the gloss step.
3. **Latency death by per-word network/LLM call** (Yomitan went *local* deinflection precisely to dodge this). Precompute/cache glosses + breakdown + TTS so a click is an instant local render, never a fresh round-trip. Click-to-explain and correction must feel <300–500ms; run correction async after the reply renders.
4. **LLM overcorrection** (arXiv 2509.20811; worse for *advanced* learners and on word/phrase choice — LLMs "improve" correct text into fluent-but-different text, destroying the pedagogical signal). Minimal-edit, recall-preserving, **target-span-only** correction; a mandatory verification pass ("is the original actually *wrong*, or just non-preferred?").
5. **Confidently-wrong grammar advice** (TalkPal; Duolingo's admitted human-review burden). A solo project cannot human-review every explanation — keep correction scope narrow, prefer rule-lookup tools (LanguageTool as a cheap deterministic cross-check) over free-generation, let users flag.
6. **Punishing legitimate L1 fallback.** At an early blend stage, an English word used because the learner doesn't yet know the L2 one is *strategic scaffolding, not an error* (translanguaging). Punishing it raises the affective filter and teaches learners to write less. Correction must be **blend-aware**: the current stage decides what even counts as an error.
7. **ASR over-leniency → false mastery** (Speak gives perfect scores on mispronounced/word-reversed input). If speaking is added later, never trust a single fluency score.
8. **Avatar / voice-clone gimmickry** (Praktika lip-sync, "generic for advanced"). Spend on correctness and click latency, not 3D faces. Persona *voice* without the avatar cost.
9. **Permanent transliteration crutch** (Du Chinese pinyin-always critique). Tie furigana/pinyin/inline-gloss to status so scaffolding *recedes* as words are mastered.
10. **SRS / review-debt overload** (LingQ users drown in due cards; Readlang auto-flashcards everything). Make SRS opt-in-per-word or auto-prune `Known`. Consider **in-context review** — the agent naturally re-uses a due word in a later turn — over a separate deck.
11. **Monotonic-only difficulty climb.** Translanguaging's anxiety-reduction benefit means the blend should **breathe**: if target-token error rate spikes, *drop the ratio back*. Don't only ratchet up.
12. **Whole-sentence TTS of a mixed sentence as a naive single call** (measured code-switch intelligibility cliff, −0.32; ElevenLabs/OpenAI fix language per call). Segment the sentence by language, synthesize each span in its correct locale with one identity-preserving multilingual voice (Azure), and concatenate — or defer whole-sentence playback until the sentence is mostly target.

## The gap OUR product fills

**Nobody combines the reading camp's per-word status + context glosses with the chat camp's conversation + correction, and then puts a *gradual English→target blend* on the spine.** Each existing product owns one slice:

- Reading apps (LingQ, Satori, Readlang) give rich **clickable, status-tracked text** — but the text is static and single-language, there's no conversation, and **you never write anything that gets corrected**.
- Chat tutors (Langua, Quazel, Duolingo Max) give **conversation + correction + click-to-explain** — but they're target-only immersion or gamified drills, **dropping the beginner into the deep end** with no L1 scaffold that fades.
- The two "blend-adjacent" products are deliberately shallow: **Toucan** proves graded word-density swapping is pleasant and works, but stays at isolated content-word insertion (never grammar) to avoid broken sentences; **Beelinguapp/Language Reactor** show *static* L1/L2 parallel contrast, not a *ramp*; **Satori** adapts furigana to knowledge but never shifts the language of the prose itself.

**What the combination uniquely delivers:**

1. **The blend turned into the spine, not a browser toy.** Toucan's word-swap, made coherent by an agent that generates whole sentences at a target ratio (constituent-boundary switching + a generate→validate→regenerate coherence guard) instead of find-replacing tokens. This is the hard problem Toucan dodged — and solving it is the moat.
2. **The blend driven by per-word mastery, not a global time dial.** LingQ-style status × FSRS retrievability decides *which* words are safe to render in L2, computed bottom-up as a per-word emergent property. Two learners at the "same stage" see different sentences (Satori's per-reader idea, applied to the *language of the prose* not just furigana). Mastery-gated, time-floored so it never stalls, and **able to fall as well as rise** when error rate spikes.
3. **Reading AND writing in one loop, where the blend stage governs both.** Click-to-explain handles comprehension (reading); blend-aware minimal-edit correction handles production (writing) — and the *same* blend stage decides what counts as an error and how dense glossing is. No surveyed product unifies comprehension and production under one progression model.
4. **A margin channel that blends on its own lagging schedule.** Satori "Other Notes" + Word Wise expansion + Langua's post-turn feedback, but *live* alongside the chat: L1 glosses early, migrating to L2 as the chat's blend matures (margin lags the spine by one step). The de-clutter valve that keeps the conversation reading like a real conversation, not a graded worksheet.
5. **Click-to-explain density that tracks the blend.** Early English-heavy turns have few target words to click, so the margin *invites* exploration; as density rises, auto-glossing throttles back and the learner is pushed to infer (i+1) — gloss only on click. The blend is what *creates* the unknown words, and click-to-lookup is what makes that safe; the two are co-dependent and must ship together.

**In one line:** the defensible product is *Satori's per-word, knowledge-adaptive reading experience fused with Langua's conversational correction loop, with Toucan's density dial promoted from a browser gimmick to a mastery-driven, coherence-guarded blend that is the entire spine* — a combination no one on the board ships.
